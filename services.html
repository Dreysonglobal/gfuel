BITUMEN

Bitumen, also known as asphalt, is a viscous, black, semi-solid form of petroleum used primarily in road construction and waterproofing. It is obtained through the distillation of crude oil and is valued for its adhesive and waterproofing properties.

LPG

LPG is a portable, clean-burning fuel mixture of propane and butane, stored under pressure as a liquid. It is derived from natural gas processing and crude oil refining.

UREA

Urea (CO(NH₂)₂) is a white, crystalline organic compound widely used in agriculture and industry. It is synthesized from ammonia and carbon dioxide under high pressure and temperature.

PETROLEUM COKE

Petroleum coke (petcoke) is a carbon-rich solid derived from oil refining, primarily used as a fuel and in industrial processes. It is produced by cracking heavy crude oil residues in coker units.

DIESEL FUEL

Diesel fuel is a refined petroleum product used in compression-ignition engines. Derived from crude oil through fractional distillation, it is heavier and more energy-dense than gasoline.
